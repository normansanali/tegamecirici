History of Togo’s

Established in 1971 by Tom Neumann and Gordon Reed, Togo’s has grown to become the second-largest submarine sandwich shop chain in the United States. Its inaugural store opened its doors in Marquette, Michigan, and since then, Togo’s has garnered a dedicated fan base, renowned for its mouthwatering sandwiches.

Togo’s boasts an extensive array of sandwiches, catering to all tastes. From hot pretzels to spicy buffalo chicken, turkey to pastrami, roast beef to timeless classics, their menu presents a diverse range of options. Whether you’re craving the heat of a Buffalo Chicken sandwich or the timeless appeal of a Turkey and Cheddar, Togo’s has a selection that caters to every palate.

Togo’s sandwiches come in three sizes to accommodate varying appetites: 3″ mini, 6″ regular, and 9″ large, enabling you to select the perfect portion for your hunger. For those seeking healthier alternatives, Togo’s provides a selection of salads, soups, and wraps. Salad choices range from the Santa Fe chicken salad to the Asian chicken salad and farmer’s market salad. Meanwhile, the wraps feature enticing flavors like bacon ranch chicken and Mediterranean chicken.

It’s important to be aware that menu prices at Togo’s may differ based on your location. For the most accurate and up-to-date pricing information, it is recommended to consult the official Togo’s website, Android app, iOS app, or other delivery platforms. However, it’s worth noting that the majority of Togo’s food items are generally priced below $20, ensuring that it remains an affordable choice for a flavorful and satisfying meal.

    Togo’s Franchising Details

    For those with aspirations of owning a Togo’s restaurant, the opportunity for franchising exists. With approximately 200 stores scattered across America, particularly concentrated in California and other Western States, Togo’s has a franchise model that allows individual entrepreneurs to own and operate the majority of these establishments. If the dream of running your own Togo’s restaurant beckons, exploring the franchising options could be the path to turning that dream into reality.

    Becoming a Togo’s franchisee entails meeting specific financial criteria. Prospective franchisees should have a liquid capital of $150,000 and a net worth of $300,000. A franchise fee of $30,000 is applicable, and the overall investment falls within the range of $239,700 to $501,000. Furthermore, there is a 5% royalty fee based on gross sales and an additional 3% ad royalty fee. These financial requirements outline the commitment and investment involved in becoming a Togo’s franchisee.
