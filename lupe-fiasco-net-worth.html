An American rapper, entrepreneur, record producer, singer, and songwriter, Lupe Fiasco’s net worth is a staggering $14 million.

Growing up, he listened to musical artists like Jay Z, Common, Nas, AZ, N.W.A, De La Soul, Pharrell Williams, Gang Starr, Mos Def, A Tribe Called Quest, Q-Tip, and Linkin Park. They were also his musical influence.

Lupe Fiasco | Charity

Lupe Fiasco is a charitable guy and often gets involved in non-profit work. He loves giving back to the poor and unfortunate and works for underprivileged communities.

In January of 2010, Lupe Fiasco released Resurrection, a song with another musician Kenna for Music for Relief’s charity organization. The song aimed to inspire donations for 2010 Haiti earthquake victims.

Similarly, the same year, together with several celebrities and activists, he went on an expedition to the top of Mount Kilimanjaro and documented it to highlight the water crisis.

The documentary was called “Summit on the Summit” and was made to show the problem of billions of people worldwide who lack access to safe drinking water.

In addition, Lupe operates several community initiatives like We Are M.U.R.A.L, Society of Spoken Art, Studio SV, and The Neighborhood Start-Up Fund.  

His foundation, We Are M.U.R.A.L (Magnifying Urban Realities & Affecting Lives), provides healthy meals and winter clothes to inner-city youth.

Whereas, The Neighborhood Start-Up Fund encourages deprived youth to develop entrepreneurial skills and build businesses in their communities.

Furthermore, he is an advisor for Zero Mass Water, a renewable, solar-powered initiative that wants to normalize clean access to drinking water.

Lupe Fiasco aims to generate awareness and inspiration through community efforts and charities.

Lupe Fiasco| Career

Lupe Fiasco started his career by featuring in Kanye West’s (now Ye) single, Touch The Sky, in 2005.

After the song’s success, he released his debut single ‘Kick, Push,’ followed by his 2006 debut album, Lupe Fiasco’s Food & Liquor. 

The album was critically acclaimed as it was nominated for three Grammy Awards, winning one for Best Urban/Alternative Performance.

In 2007, Fiasco dropped his second album, Lupe Fiasco’s The Cool. It was referred to as 2007’s best hip-hop album.

Lupe was on his way to release his third studio album, Laser, in 2010, but Atlantic Records controversially delayed it for lack of commercial singles. The album was later released in 2011, after fans’ continuous protest and demand.

The album was a commercial success as it debuted at number one on the Billboard 200.

FAQs

How many times has Lupe Fiasco been nominated for Grammy Awards?

Was Lupe Fiasco’s father Black Panther?

Lupe Fiasco’s father was a former Black Panther, a member of a political organization created by African Americans in the USA to fight injustice aimed at their community.
