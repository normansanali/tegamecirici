Frank Gore is an American professional football player who has represented the San Francisco 49ers (2005–2014), the Indianapolis Colts (2015–2017), the Miami Dolphins (2018), the Buffalo Bills (2019), and the New York Jets (2020) in the National Football League (NFL), as a running back. He had been picked 65th overall in the 3rd round of the 2005 NFL Draft by the San Francisco 49ers. He had been included in the Pro Bowl (the NFL‘s ‘All-Star Game’) 5 times (2006, 2009, 2011, 2012, 2013) and was named in the ‘NFL 2010s All-Decade Team’.

    Frank Gore Facts

  • Frank, during his senior year in high school (2000), had broken several Dade County single-season records, including rushing yards (2,997) and touchdowns (39).
  • In his 2nd year at the University of Miami (2002), he had suffered a torn anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) in his left knee during spring practice and had been forced to spend the entire season recovering from knee surgery.
  • In 2016, he had been honored with the Art Rooney Award, presented annually by the NFL in recognition of outstanding sportsmanship on the playing field.
  • As of the end of October 2021, he had held the record for the most number of games played by a running back (241) in the NFL. He is also the first player in NFL history with 12 consecutive 1,200-yards seasons from scrimmage.
  • As of the end of October 2021, he had also held the NFL records for the most number of consecutive seasons with at least 500 yards rushing (16) and the most number of consecutive seasons with at least 600 yards rushing (14).
  • On April 12, 2018, he was given the ‘Keys to the City’ by the mayor of Miami. The mayor had also proclaimed April 12 as ‘Frank Gore Day’.
