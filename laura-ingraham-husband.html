Laura Ingraham Career

Laura Ingraham has had a successful and diverse career in media, law, and politics.

She graduated from Dartmouth College with a degree in history in 1985.

She then earned her law degree from the University of Virginia School of Law in 1991.

Ingraham started her career as a notes editor for The Dartmouth Review, a conservative student newspaper.

She then worked as a speechwriter for the Reagan administration and as a judicial clerk for Judge Ralph K. Winter Jr. of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit.

She also practiced law at Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom, where she specialized in white-collar criminal defense and First Amendment issues.

Later she entered the media industry in 1996, when she became a CBS commentator and hosted Watch It! on MSNBC.

She then launched her own radio show, The Laura Ingraham Show, which ran from 2001 to 2018.

It was one of the most popular radio shows in the country, reaching millions of listeners.

She also became a regular contributor and guest host for Fox News Channel, appearing on shows like The O’Reilly Factor, Hannity & Colmes, and Fox & Friends.
