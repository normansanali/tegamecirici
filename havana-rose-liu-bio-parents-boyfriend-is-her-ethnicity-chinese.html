Havana Rose Liu On No Exit

Hulu premiered the thriller movie No Exit which is directed by Damien Power. The show wrapped up its production in New Zealand in June 2021. The film was produced by Scott Frank, co-creator of The Queen’s Gambit. Whereas, the show was written by Andrew Barrer and Gabriel Ferrari.

The movie tells of a young woman named Darby (played by actress Havana Rose Liu) stuck with a group of people at a mountain rest top during a blizzard. Then things take a turn for danger when Darby discovers a kidnapped child in a car belonging to one of the people inside, setting in motion a terrifying life-or-death struggle to discover who among them is the kidnapper.

Havana performed alongside actors such as Dennis Haysbert, Danny Ramirez, David Rysdahl, Dale Dickey, and Mila Harris.

Speaking with Looper, Havana shared that the script, the character, and also the director was the reason she decided to audition for “No Exit”.

Meanwhile, talking about her character with Screen Rant, Liu shared, “I think she’s bold and gritty, and complex and powerful, yet also very nuanced and vulnerable, and has a quieter power that makes her interesting. In falling in love with her, it just felt like, “Oh, I want to do this part, even if it’s terrifying.” The journey she goes on, spiritually in a way, is even more important than me [having] to get over my fear of fear.”

Havana also shared that since her character was an addict she did the research for the character. She opened up about having family members suffering from the same problem. While doing research, she also developed a deep sense of compassion for what people who have experiences with addiction either current, past, present are going through.

Who Are Havana Rose Liu Parents?

Havana Rose Liu was born to her XO Group (currently valued at over $500 million) formerly known as The Knot founders David Liu (@davdliu) and Carley Roney (@carleyroney). Her parents met each other as undergraduate film and television students at New York University but it took them eight years to fall in love. Carley was a freshman, David was a senior

Speaking with Business Insider her father said, “While we didn’t spend much time talking to each other, we probably saw each other every weekend for a year”. Whereas her mother chimed in, “Yah, and we spent two weeks together in the Soviet Union [through NYU] in which we exchanged exactly one word.”

Years later, they ran into each other at a party then they were inseparable. Within seven months of dating, they tied the knots on 10 July 1993. Because of the rushed wedding, Havana’s grandmother thought that her mother was pregnant but she wasn’t.

By 1995, Havana’s parents started a business venture making CD-Roms for brands like the Smithsonian. Then in 1996, they founded The Knot. Read more about their career here.

Havana has two brothers; Luis and Dublin. Her paternal grandfather is Richard Liu (@richard.liu.507464).
